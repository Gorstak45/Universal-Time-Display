Understanding the Universal Format

This new universal time and date format is designed to be globally applicable, removing the need for time zones and seasonal adjustments. It consists of three parts:

  • Year: The number of years since January 1, 2000. For example, 24 represents the year 2024.
  • Day: The total number of days passed since January 1, 2000. For instance, 5023 indicates the 5,023rd day since this starting date.
  • Decimal Time: A decimal fraction of the current day, where 0.00 represents midnight UTC, 0.50 represents noon UTC, and 0.99 approaches midnight UTC again.

So, a universal time of 24.5023.83 would mean it is the year 2024, the 5,023rd day since January 1, 2000, and 83% through the current day (around 19:55 UTC).
